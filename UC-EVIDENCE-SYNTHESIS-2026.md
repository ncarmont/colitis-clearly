# Ulcerative Colitis Evidence Synthesis — Updated March 2026

## Executive Summary

UC is a relapsing-remitting inflammatory disease where outcomes depend on whether mucosal inflammation is fully suppressed. Contemporary targets: steroid-free clinical remission + objective remission (endoscopic ± histologic), supported by faecal calprotectin (FC).

### Three Dominant Flare Risk Drivers:
1. **Uncontrolled "silent" inflammation** — FC ≥250 µg/g vs <50: aHR 3.25 for objective flare (PREdiCCt cohort)
2. **Medication non-adherence** — HR 5.5 for relapse in quiescent UC (PMID: 12543288)
3. **Enteric superinfection** — CDI: colectomy OR ~1.90; CMV antivirals reduce colectomy OR ~0.20

### Headline Effect Sizes:
- FC ≥250 vs <50 predicts objective flare: **aHR 3.25**
- Mesalamine non-adherence predicts relapse: **HR 5.5**
- Upadacitinib induction week 8: **26–34% vs 4–5%**; maintenance week 52: **42–52% vs 12%** (NNT ≈ 3–5)
- Tofacitinib induction week 8: **16.6–18.5% vs 3.6–8.2%**; maintenance: **34–41% vs 11%** (NNT ≈ 8–12)
- Ozanimod induction week 10: **18.4% vs 6.0%**; maintenance: **37.0% vs 18.5%** (NNT ≈ 8)
- Mirikizumab induction week 12: **24.2% vs 13.3%**; maintenance: **49.9% vs 25.1%** (NNT ≈ 9)
- Risankizumab induction week 12: **20.3% vs 6.2%**; maintenance: **~38–40% vs 25.1%** (NNT ≈ 7)

- ECCO therapeutics guideline for UC (2022)
- AGA living guideline on moderate-to-severe UC (Dec 2024)

## Ranked Negative Factors (Worst for UC)

| Rank | Factor | Evidence | Effect Size |
|------|--------|----------|-------------|
| 1 | Medication non-adherence (5-ASA) | High | HR 5.5 for relapse |
| 2 | Elevated FC while "in remission" | High | aHR 3.25 (FC ≥250 vs <50) |
| 3 | C. difficile superinfection | High | Colectomy OR ~1.90 |
| 4 | CMV reactivation (steroid-refractory) | Moderate-High | Steroid refractoriness ~2.34×; antivirals OR 0.20 |
| 5 | High meat intake during remission | Moderate | aHR 1.95 (highest vs lowest quartile) |
| 6 | Delayed escalation for mod-severe | Moderate | Preventable ongoing inflammation |
| 7 | High systemic corticosteroid exposure | High | Metabolic/infectious complications |
| 8 | Psychological stress/distress | Low-Moderate | Variable; stress precedes exacerbation |
| 9 | Poor vaccination before immunosuppression | High (guideline) | Preventable infectious morbidity |
| 10 | NSAID exposure | Low-Moderate | Inconsistent association in meta-analysis |
| 11 | Pro-inflammatory food additives (carrageenan) | Low | Small RCT suggests increased relapse |
| 12 | Vitamin D deficiency | Low-Moderate | IBD RCT meta-analysis: relapse RR 0.64 with supplementation |

## Ranked Positive Factors (Best for UC)

| Rank | Factor | Evidence | Effect Size |
|------|--------|----------|-------------|
| 1 | Adherence to maintenance therapy | High | Removes HR 5.5 relapse hazard; 5-ASA RR 0.68 |
| 2 | Treat-to-target monitoring (FC + endoscopy) | High | FC-based risk stratification: aHR 3.25 prediction |
| 3 | 5-ASA at adequate dose (oral ± topical) | High | Induction RR 0.86; Maintenance relapse RR 0.68 |
| 4 | Advanced therapies (appropriately selected) | High | Induction +10-30%; Maintenance +15-40% |
| 5 | Rapid ASUC management | High | IV steroids → day 3 decision → rescue/surgery |
| 6 | Budesonide MMX | Moderate | Remission ~15% vs 7% at week 8 |
| 7 | Mediterranean diet pattern | Moderate | FC reduction over 12 weeks |
| 8 | Curcumin adjunct to mesalamine | Moderate | RCT evidence for remission |
| 9 | FMT for active UC | Moderate | Meta-analysis: clinical/endoscopic benefit |
| 10 | E. coli Nissle 1917 maintenance | Moderate | Non-inferior to mesalazine (~36% vs ~34% relapse) |
| 11 | Vaccination optimization | High (guideline) | Reduces preventable infections |
| 12 | Vitamin D repletion | Low-Moderate | IBD relapse RR 0.64; UC subgroup underpowered |

## Response Timeline
- Hours to days: IV corticosteroids in ASUC
- Day 3: Rescue decision point (Oxford criteria)
- Days to weeks: JAK inhibitors (3-14 days)
- 1-3 weeks: Oral/rectal 5-ASA
- 4-8 weeks: 5-ASA/budesonide formal induction; Anti-TNF window
- 6-12 weeks: Vedolizumab/IL-23/S1P induction
- 3-6 months: Biochemical remission target
- 6-12 months: Endoscopic improvement target
