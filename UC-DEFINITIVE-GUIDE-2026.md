# The Definitive Evidence-Based Guide to Ulcerative Colitis

Ulcerative colitis can be driven into deep remission — and in some cases drug-free remission — through a combination of advanced targeted therapies, microbiome restoration, dietary optimization, and psychological support. The research landscape has transformed dramatically between 2023 and 2026, with 14+ advanced therapies now available, network meta-analyses clarifying which drugs work best, and landmark trials validating interventions once considered fringe (therapeutic appendectomy, fecal microbiota transplantation, curcumin).

This report synthesizes evidence from hundreds of RCTs, meta-analyses, and systematic reviews.

## Pipeline Drugs (2026–2028)
- **Tulisokibart** (anti-TL1A, Merck): 26% vs 1% remission; first companion diagnostic in UC; Phase 3 ATLAS-UC
- **Obefazimod** (miR-124 enhancer, Abivax): 16.4% remission; NDA filing H2 2026
- **Icotrokinra** (oral IL-23R peptide): 63.5% response Phase 2b
- **Dual targeted therapy**: VEGA trial — guselkumab + golimumab = 83% response vs 61–75% monotherapy

## Microbiome Insights
- UC patients: reduced F. prausnitzii and Roseburia, increased Proteobacteria, butyrate drop >2-fold (SMD -2.09)
- Multi-strain probiotics: OR 3.85 for remission (NMA of 20 RCTs)
- VSL#3: 42.9% vs 15.7% placebo remission
- Probiotics: 66% lower relapse in maintenance (OR 0.34)
- Super-donor FMT: high Lachnospiraceae/Oscillospiraceae, absent Fusobacteria/R. gnavus

## Deep Remission
- Only ~37% in clinical remission have endoscopic + histological remission
- FC <100 µg/g correlates with histologic remission
- Drug-free remission: 21% achieved long-term after anti-TNF discontinuation
- 81–88% of relapsers respond to reintroduction
